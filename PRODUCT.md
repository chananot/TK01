# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Direct-to-consumer running-shoe shoppers in Thailand. They browse on mobile first, pay in THB, and expect fast, trustworthy checkout with clear delivery and returns. Two overlapping motivations: performance runners who care about the tech (foam, plate, drop, weight) and streetwear-minded buyers who buy on look and culture. They arrive from social/paid traffic onto a single page and decide quickly whether the brand feels credible and worth the price.

The job to be done: see the shoe, believe the performance claim, trust the store, and buy without leaving the page.

## Product Purpose

VELOCITY is a single-page brand storefront for premium performance running shoes, launching with the Aeroflow 01. The page has to carry the entire funnel: establish the brand, sell the product's engineering, build trust with a Thai audience, and close the sale through an in-page cart, search, and quick-view flow. Success is a shopper who lands from an ad and completes a purchase in one session, feeling they bought from a real, premium brand rather than a marketplace listing.

## Brand Personality

Premium but approachable, rooted in running culture and street style. Three words: **bold, credible, human.** The voice is confident and direct, never corporate or hype-driven. It respects the buyer's intelligence: specific about the tech, honest about price and returns, warm in tone. It should feel like a brand a runner would wear off the track, not a generic athletic megabrand and not a discount listing.

Emotional goal: forward motion and confidence. The buyer should feel faster and more capable for choosing VELOCITY, and secure that the store is legitimate.

## Anti-references

- **Generic Nike/Adidas clone.** No safe corporate athletic template: stock hero photo over a polished, evenly-spaced product grid. The layout must have its own point of view.
- **Cream/beige AI-editorial.** No warm-neutral "editorial restraint" default (sand/paper backgrounds, tiny tracked all-caps eyebrows above every section). That reads as AI slop, not a running brand.
- **Cluttered discount marketplace.** No Lazada/Shopee density: no discount stickers, slashed-price badges, countdown clutter, or busy promo rails. Premium and composed, not a bargain bin.
- **Soft pastel wellness/lifestyle.** No calm pastel spa aesthetic. This brand is loud, high-contrast, and kinetic.

## Design Principles

- **Speed is the feeling.** Every choice (typography weight, motion curve, contrast) should communicate forward motion. The page itself should feel fast, not just claim it.
- **Earn the price.** Premium is proven through craft, specificity, and restraint in the right places, not through decoration. Show the engineering; don't just assert "performance."
- **Trust is the close.** For a Thai DTC audience, credibility signals (clear THB pricing, delivery, returns, reviews, secure checkout) are load-bearing UX, not afterthoughts. Never bury them.
- **One loud voice, used sparingly.** The volt-green signature accent earns its impact through rarity. When everything shouts, nothing does.
- **Culture over category.** Lean into running culture and street style to differentiate from megabrand templates; the brand should feel worn, not just sold.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. High-contrast body text against every background (≥4.5:1), visible focus rings on all interactive elements, full keyboard operation for the cart drawer, search dialog, and quick-view modal, and a working `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for all motion. The signature volt-green (#c9ff12) is bright; never place light or muted text on it, and never rely on color alone to convey stock, selection, or error state.
